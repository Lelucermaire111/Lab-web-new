Channel Measurements, Modeling, and Application


1. Comprehensive channel measurements and analysis and accurate channel dataset: Our team carries out comprehensive channel measurements in complex communication scenarios [1]. Meanwhile, with the help of the commercial platform, i.e., Wireless InSite, accurate wireless communication channel simulation datasets are obtained based on ray-tracing technology. By analyzing the comprehensive measured and ray-tracing-based channel data, new channel characteristics and physical features are revealed [2]. Furthermore, the obtained measured and ray-tracing-based channel data can be exploited to validate the developed channel models [1], [3].

2. Realistic and easy-to-use channel models: Our team focuses on developing realistic and easy-to-use channel models for diverse communication scenarios, including vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V), unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), and satellite communications [3]-[6]. Also, the developed channel model covers multiple frequency bands, utilizes various channel modeling approaches, and can support the design of both communication and integrated sensing and communication (ISAC) systems. In the developed channel models, typical channel characteristics, i.e., channel non-stationarity in the space-time-frequency domain, inherent channel physical features, i.e., channel consistency in the time-space domain, vehicular traffic density (VTD), and continuously arbitrary trajectories of transceivers are considered and captured adequately [5]-[8]. On the one hand, the proposed channel model can be utilized as a simulation and validation platform, which is a theoretical foundation of 6G communication system design and technology development. On the other hand, our team develops more channel modeling approaches, e.g., AI-based approach, to promote the development of channel modeling theory research.

3. In-depth research on channel application: Based on the proper channel measurement and modeling, our team further conducts research on channel application in complex high-mobility communication scenarios. Specifically, the impacts on VTD, UAV-related parameters, and channel space-time-frequency non-stationarity on the network-level simulation, i.e., average downloading percentage, are investigated [9], [10]. The interesting observation can provide useful guidance for the successful design of communication networks in complex high-mobility scenarios. Furthermore, the developed channel model can also support various channel applications, including ISAC and high precision localization.
