Waveform Design for Integrated Sensing and Communication Towards the Future of Ubiquitous Sensing and Mobile Communications


1. Waveform design in highly dynamic scenarios: The recent convergence of IP networks and IT clouds is fueling the emergence of large-scale distributed computing networks that can host content and applications close to information sources and end users, providing rapid response, analysis, and delivery of augmented information in real time. This enables augmented information services, including automation services for smart physical systems, augmented experience services and network services. We characterize the capacity region of distributed computing networks under communication and computation resource constraints, and develop the first throughput-optimal routing and scheduling algorithm for unicast, multicast and broadcast flows in distributed computing networks under stochastic arrivals.

2. Waveform design in wideband mmWave ISAC: Network flows in a distributed computing network rely on both communication resources for transmission and computation resources for processing. The unavailability of either type of resources may lead to the failure to support flows. We study the max flow and min cut in distributed computing networks. Unlike the classical communication networks where max flow is the same as min cut, in distributed computing networks, min cut can be at most twice as large as max flow. We study flow interdiction problems to characterize the survivability of distributed computing networks.

3. Hardware platform for verification: Datacenter traditionally has Clos topology which support non-blocking communication between machines. The communication patterns between aggregation blocks are relatively predictable and far from the worst-case traffic matrix under typical job scheduling policies. Direct connect between aggregation blocks with traffic and topology engineering jointly optimizes inter-block routing and topology, and achieves shorter and more efficient paths. The new architecture reduces capex by 30% and reduces power by 40%.
