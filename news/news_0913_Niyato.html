Prof. Dusit Niyato from Nanyang Technological University
presented a series of academic lectures online


  During August 23-26, 2022, our team held a series of academic lectures online on wireless communication and networks. These lectures were presented by Prof. Dusit Niyato (IEEE Fellow and IET Fellow) from the School of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. These lectures were held by Prof. Xiang Cheng and Prof. Jianan Zhang. Teachers and students in our team actively participated in these lectures and had adequate discussions.

  The first lecture (2022.08.23, 2:00-4:00 p.m., Beijing time) contained two parts, 1) Semantic Communication (SemCom) Meets Edge Intelligence, and 2) Distributed Machine Learning (DML) for 6G Networks. The first part gave background and presented the framework of the modern SemCom. The latter part of the lecture provided an overview of DML techniques and their recent advances to address practical challenges in 6G networks.

  This second lecture (2022.08.24, 2:00-4:00 p.m., Beijing time) contained two parts, 1) Metaverse Virtual Service Management: Game Theoretic Approaches, and 2) Borrowing Arrows with Thatched Boats: The Art of Defeating Reactive Jammers in IoT Networks. In the first part, Prof. Dusit Niyato introduced concepts of Metaverse and virtual service management. The latter part of the lecture presented a novel deception strategy, which is inspired by the ''Borrowing Arrows with Thatched Boats'' strategy.

  The third lecture (2022.08.26, 3:00-5:00 p.m., Beijing time) contained two parts, 1) Resource Allocation in Coded Distributed Computing, and 2) Deep Reinforcement Learning and Its Applications for Future Wireless Networks. In the first part, he discussed the motivation of coded distributed computing (CDC), reviewed several CDC works, and presented the applications of CDC schemes. The latter part of the lecture provided a background of deep reinforcement learning (DRL), and recent advances in DRL to address practical challenges in wireless networks.
