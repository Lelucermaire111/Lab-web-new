Prof. Wei Zhang from The University of New South Wales presented a series of academic lectures online


  During September 14-16, 2022, our group held a series of academic lectures online on wireless communication and networks. These lectures were presented by Prof. Wei Zhang (IEEE Fellow, Vice President of IEEE Communications Society) from the School of Electrical Engineering and Telecommunications, the University of New South Wales, Sydney, Australia. These lectures were held by Prof. Xiang Cheng. Teachers and students in our group actively participated in these lectures and had adequate discussions..

  The first lecture (2022.09.14, 3:00-5:00 p.m., Beijing time) A Combinatorial Auction Resource Trading Mechanism for Cybertwin-Based 6G Network contained three parts, 1) Cloud Native Network, 2) Cybertwin based Cloud Native Network, 3) Distributed Combinatorial Auction. The first part introduced the Cloud Native Network and clarified some differences from Cloud Native Computing. The second part introduced Cybertwin based Cloud Native Network. The third part presented a new algorithm called distributed combinatorial auction mechanism.

  The second lecture (2022.09.15, 2:30-4:30 p.m., Beijing time) Millimeter Communications for UAV Systems begins with an introduction to the applications of UAVs. In this lecture, Prof. Wei Zhang overviewed some research challenges and opportunities in developing mmWave communications for UAVs, including 3D beam tracking, dynamic routing, trajectory design, and UAV networking technologies, etc.

  The third lecture (2022.09.16, 2:00-4:00 p.m., Beijing time) Intelligent Reflecting Surfaces Assisted Millimeter Wave Communications presented a joint beam training and positioning approach for IRSs assisted mmWave communications, including an introduction of Intelligent Reflecting Surface (IRS), research motivation, system model and problem formulation, beaming training and positioning method. Prof. Wei Zhang also presented the numerical results to verify the benefits of the proposed approach and gave some directions for future research work.

Fig. 1. The lecture titled Millimetre Wave Communications for UAV.

  Prof. Zhang Wei's excellent lectures have introduced many frontier research topics which aroused great interest among teachers and students. After each lecture, we held a Q & A part in which Prof. Zhang answered the questions from students. The questions include but not limited to basic theories, technology applications, algorithms, and program improvements.
